Mental and Physical Health

988 Lifeline

The 988 Lifeline is available 24/7 for free, confidential support. Whether you're dealing with mental health struggles, substance use concerns, or just need someone to talk to, caring counselors are always there. You're not alone.

Clark Counseling and Health Center

Clark offers students free, confidential mental health counseling with licensed providers, plus low-cost medical care with a naturopathic doctor. Our clinicians can also support you with academic concerns.

Coffee And Connection Time

A cozy drop-in space to grab coffee, connect with others, and start your week with support. No appointment needed, just come as you are, hosted by a Clark College counselor.

Neurodivergent Student Support Circle

Join a supportive circle of neurodivergent Clark students to make friends, check in, and share tips for school and life. It's open to anyone with diagnosed or suspected neurodivergence. Come weekly to build community, or drop in whenever you can.

Student Health Services

The Counseling & Health Center provides low-cost medical services such as physical or reproductive health exams, immunizations, lab testing and wellness consultations to Clark students.

Support

Basic Needs Program

If you are a Clark College student who is facing challenges with access to adequate food, stable housing, affordable childcare, or meeting other basic needs, you can fill out their Basic Needs Intake Form. Once an intake form is submitted, a Basic Needs Team Member will reach out with personalized support.

Emergency Grants

Available to enrolled students who've completed at least one term and are facing an unexpected financial emergency. Funds are limited, not guaranteed, and can't be used for tuition or ongoing expenses. The goal is to help cover short-term gaps, connect students with support, and keep them enrolled.

Food Pantry

Items vary week to week by availability, but may include: fresh produce, meat, bread and pastries, pantry staples, and more.

Passport To Careers

This program helps former foster youth and unaccompanied homeless youth access scholarships, financial aid, and dedicated support services to begin and complete their college journey.

Veterans Center of Excellence

The Clark College Veterans Center of Excellence coordinates flexible and comprehensive support all veteran, active duty, and military affiliated students.

Workforce Education Services (WES)

Workforce Education Services (WES) administers a variety of programs that are designed to support students.
