About Us

Ashley

Researched and wrote up impacts of financial struggles on student mental health.

Ashley is a 31-year-old student pursuing an associate's degree in medical Billing and Coding and Health Management. Through her studies, she is learning how financial systems impact healthcare access, patient care, and administrative efficiency. Her goal is to understand how financial management plays a key role in supporting healthcare organizations and improving services for patients. She is passionate about building skills that allow her to contribute meaningfully to healthcare operations while ensuring systems run smoothly and ethically.

This field is important to her because it aligns with her career goals, provides stability for her future, and allows her to grow professionally. It is also personally meaningful to her because she understands firsthand how financial struggles can affect college students, including stress, limited resources, and challenges in balancing education with financial responsibilities. Experiencing or witnessing these challenges has motivated her to pursue a career that promotes financial awareness, stability, and support within healthcare and education systems.

 

Chris

Created and coded the website, supported other group members when needed, and researched statistics relating to financial struggles.

Chris is an Accounting major currently working to finish his associates degree. He has been a student at Clark College for over five years now, as he previously earned a degree in Web Development, but was unable to utilize it due to strong and continuing fluctuations in that particular job market. As a result, he has steered away from technology focused careers as his greater strength is in math and organization.

In his free time, Chris enjoys a wide variety of hobbies, but most notably writing and crocheting. There is nothing he enjoys more than just having something he can build with his own two hands.

The topic of financial struggles is something that Chris is very passionate about as his family didn't have a lot of money to work with growing up. He got very lucky however, and his parents were both able to find great jobs that paid well during his teen years, but most people aren't that lucky. It is very important to him that those who are struggling right now be able to acquire the resources they need to get their head above water. Many of his friends are dealing with these sorts of struggles every day and he wants to do whatever he can to help whoever he can.

 

Kristin

Researched and located current online financial resources for Clark College students and gaps present in housing support. Also created the logo.

I'm a Clark College student finishing my AA in Accounting. This topic was important to me because, between rising costs of just about everything, limited student housing options, and the challenge of balancing school with work, it's clear that many students are doing everything “right” and still falling behind.

As someone who values practical solutions, I wanted to focus on the parts of this issue that students can actually use right now: discounts, resources, and support programs that reduce costs and make school more manageable. I also identified the housing gap and possible options the college could explore. Just a note: while I love to search out a great deal, I am not a financial or housing expert, so students should contact qualified professionals or Student Services for additional assistance!

 

Je'von

Researched and wrote up financial struggles related to those who have been incarcerated.

Je'von Williams is a multifaceted scholar-athlete whose intellectual rigor and athletic prowess have distinguished him both in and out of the classroom. Currently pursuing a major in Psychology, Je'von has cultivated a deep understanding of human behavior, cognitive processes, and the dynamics of social interaction, demonstrating an insatiable curiosity and commitment to academic excellence.

Athletically, Je'von has an extraordinary track record: he was an All-American football star and earned the prestigious Valon Victorian Award during his high school career, exemplifying leadership, perseverance, and strategic acuity on the field. His competitive spirit extends beyond athletics; in 2012, Je'von achieved recognition as a Chess Champion, showcasing exceptional analytical thinking, foresight, and problem-solving abilities.

Je'von's unique combination of academic achievement, athletic distinction, and strategic intellect positions him as a driven and dynamic individual, prepared to make impactful contributions in any professional or scholarly endeavor.

As a student with a criminal record and limited financial resources, these issues are not abstract—they are real challenges. Without family support, savings, or external resources, financial aid becomes essential for continuing my education. However, eligibility restrictions, lack of awareness, and systemic barriers can make it harder to access assistance. Education is one of the most powerful tools for transformation and stability, but only if institutions provide equitable access to funding and support services.

The importance of these issues lies in opportunity. When financial systems are inclusive and supportive, justice-involved students can succeed academically, build careers, and contribute positively to society. Expanding access to aid, improving transparency about eligibility, and reducing policy barriers can significantly improve outcomes for students like him.
